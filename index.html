About

Modern neural networks are powerful, but their scale can hide substantial computational and memory cost. My work asks how we can turn that redundancy into systems that are structurally and computationally optimal.

I am an M.S. in Artificial Intelligence student at the University of Southern California, supervised by Prof. Sai Praneeth Karimireddy and Prof. Vatsal Sharan. I also work with the Large-Scale Machine Learning Systems Lab at Inha University.

My research connects theory, optimization, and practical large-scale experiments to make models lighter, more robust, and more efficient to reason with.
